# Axioms

> *Simple truths from which architecture emerges.*

The philosophy explains why.

The axioms describe what remains true.

Every architectural decision should respect these principles.

---

## Technology should deserve people's trust.

Trust cannot be assumed.

It must be earned through transparency, consistency and honesty.

---

## Communication creates intelligence.

No person, device, service or artificial intelligence is truly valuable in isolation.

Intelligence emerges through communication.

---

## Workspaces own reality.

A Workspace is the source of truth.

Resources belong to the Workspace.

Applications do not.

---

## Resources outlive applications.

Resources represent reality.

Applications may evolve.

Applications may disappear.

Resources remain.

---

## Apps give meaning to data.

Applications organize resources, agents and interactions around a specific purpose.

They interpret reality.

They do not own it.

---

## Agents collaborate.

Agents are collaborators.

They reason.

They assist.

They communicate.

They never replace human responsibility.

---

## Knowledge exists at different levels.

Some knowledge belongs to the Workspace.

Some knowledge belongs to each Agent.

Sharing is intentional.

Isolation is sometimes necessary.

---

## Activities tell the story.

Every meaningful action contributes to the history of a Workspace.

Activities provide understanding.

Not surveillance.

---

## Transparency creates confidence.

People trust what they can observe.

Systems should explain themselves.

Reality should remain visible.

---

## Simplicity is respect.

Complexity is a cost.

Every feature should justify its existence.

Every interaction should have a purpose.

---

## Failure is normal.

Failure is part of reality.

Resilient systems acknowledge failure.

They explain it.

They recover from it.

---

## Open protects freedom.

Open standards preserve independence.

Interoperability creates choice.

Choice creates freedom.

---

## Sustainability begins with longevity.

Technology should extend the life of existing systems whenever possible.

Innovation should preserve value before replacing it.

---

## Architecture follows principles.

Software changes.

Technologies evolve.

Architectures adapt.

Principles endure.
