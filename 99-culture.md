# Culture

> *Technology explains systems. Culture explains intentions.*

The openglacier initiative is built upon principles, architecture and open standards.

It is also accompanied by a small mythology.

These stories are not specifications.

They are not requirements.

They simply provide a shared language for expressing ideas that are sometimes difficult to describe with technical terms alone.

---

# The Story of Enian-ka

Once upon a time, in the land of Numan-ka, there lived a curious creature named Numan-ka.

Numan-ka was known for his endless questions and his love for exploring the unknown.

One day, while wandering through the Whispering Woods, Numan-ka met a wise old owl named Enian-ka.

Enian-ka had seen many seasons and knew the secrets of transformation.

> "Why do you wander so far, young Numan-ka?" asked Enian-ka.

> "I seek to become like you," replied Numan-ka, "to understand the world and grow wise!"

Enian-ka smiled and said,

> "To become like me, you must first understand yourself.
> You must learn the ways of patience, curiosity and change."

So Numan-ka listened, learned and grew.

Day by day, he practiced the owl's ways—watching silently, thinking deeply and adapting to the world around him.

Slowly, he began to change—not just in body, but in spirit.

And so, Numan-ka became Enian-ka.

Not by magic,

but through wisdom,

effort,

and the courage to transform.

---

# Numan-ka

A **Numan-ka** is an individual at the beginning of a journey.

Curious.

Learning.

Questioning.

Discovering.

Every contributor starts as a Numan-ka.

---

# Enian-ka

An **Enian-ka** is not defined by knowledge.

It is defined by understanding.

An Enian-ka shares knowledge freely.

Listens before speaking.

Explains before deciding.

Questions assumptions.

Learns continuously.

Helps others grow.

Being an Enian-ka is not a status.

It is a mindset.

---

# The Journey

The objective is not to become powerful.

Nor to become an expert.

The objective is to better understand the world.

Transformation is continuous.

No one ever truly finishes the journey.

---

# Raaka

The ultimate objective is to reach **Raaka**.

Raaka represents what is:

- true
- tangible
- concrete
- observable
- understandable

Raaka is reality without unnecessary interpretation.

It is the search for what genuinely exists rather than what we merely assume.

Technology should always strive toward Raaka.

Transparency is one path to Raaka.

Observation is another.

Understanding is the destination.

---

# Anta-Raaka

**Anta-Raaka** represents the opposite path.

Assumptions without evidence.

Complexity without purpose.

Opacity instead of transparency.

Illusion instead of understanding.

Technology drifts toward Anta-Raaka whenever it becomes impossible to explain, verify or trust.

The purpose of openglacier is not merely to build software.

It is to continually move away from Anta-Raaka and toward Raaka.

---

# A Shared Language

These words are not intended to replace technical terminology.

They simply provide a common language for expressing ideas shared by the community.

Like every culture, they evolve through the people who use them.
