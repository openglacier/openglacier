# Missions

> *Missions transform intentions into outcomes.*

## What is a mission?

A mission is a coordinated objective carried out by individuals, agents and resources.

A mission exists to achieve a meaningful outcome.

It defines *why* something is being done.

Not *how*.

---

## Missions are objects

Every mission is represented as an object within the workspace.

Like every other object, a mission has:

- an identity
- a presence
- a state
- relationships
- events
- actions
- history

Missions follow the same object model as every other entity in the ecosystem.

---

## Purpose before process

Traditional workflows define sequences of steps.

A mission defines an objective.

How that objective is achieved may evolve over time.

The purpose remains.

---

## Missions coordinate collaboration

A mission brings together the right participants.

These may include:

- individuals
- organizations
- agents
- resources

Every participant contributes according to its capabilities.

---

## Missions orchestrate capabilities

A mission does not perform actions itself.

Instead, it coordinates the capabilities provided by participating resources and agents.

Resources provide capabilities.

Agents execute actions.

Individuals make decisions.

The mission gives direction.

---

## Missions evolve

Objectives rarely remain static.

Requirements change.

Participants change.

Resources become available or unavailable.

A mission adapts while preserving its purpose.

---

## Missions are observable

Every mission should answer simple questions.

What is the objective?

Who is participating?

What is happening now?

What has already been completed?

What remains to be done?

Transparency enables collaboration.

---

## Missions communicate

A mission continuously emits meaningful events.

Examples include:

- mission created
- participant joined
- resource assigned
- objective completed
- mission paused

These events allow the ecosystem to react and collaborate.

---

## Missions preserve context

A mission remembers its history.

It records decisions.

It records participation.

It records outcomes.

Understanding why something happened is often more valuable than knowing that it happened.

---

## Missions remain independent

A mission should not depend on a specific technology.

Replacing an AI model.

Changing a storage provider.

Migrating an email service.

None of these should redefine the mission.

Technology supports the mission.

It never defines it.

---

## Missions may span ecosystems

A mission is not limited to one device or one service.

It may coordinate resources distributed across multiple environments.

The ecosystem behaves as one.

---

## Human responsibility

Agents assist.

Resources provide capabilities.

Individuals remain responsible for the mission and its outcome.

Responsibility always remains identifiable.

---

## Missions conclude

Every mission eventually reaches an outcome.

It may succeed.

It may fail.

It may be cancelled.

Completion does not erase its history.

Every mission contributes to understanding the ecosystem.
