# Principles

> *Good principles outlive technologies.*

These principles guide every decision within the openglacier initiative.

When technology evolves, principles remain.

---

## Trust before features

Technology should deserve people's trust before earning their attention.

---

## Transparency before automation

People should understand what systems do before asking them to automate anything.

---

## Communication creates intelligence

Intelligence emerges from meaningful interactions, not from isolated systems.

---

## Simplicity is a feature

Complexity is a cost.

Reduce it whenever possible.

---

## Build less, connect more

Existing technologies already solve many problems.

Prefer connecting them over replacing them.

---

## Sustainability is innovation

The most sustainable technology is often the one that already exists.

---

## Longevity over novelty

Reliable technologies deserve a long life.

Newer is not always better.

---

## Open by design

Open standards create stronger ecosystems than proprietary protocols.

---

## Neutrality empowers choice

Technology should not decide which services people use.

People decide.

---

## Freedom through interoperability

Users should always be free to replace technologies without rebuilding their ecosystem.

---

## Privacy by architecture

Privacy should emerge naturally from the way systems are designed.

---

## Security through simplicity

Simple systems are easier to understand, audit and secure.

---

## Explainability matters

Every important action should be understandable.

Technology should never become a black box.

---

## Honest systems build confidence

Failures should be visible.

Success should be measurable.

Technology should never pretend.

---

## Failure is information

Failures are part of reality.

Good systems recover instead of hiding them.

---

## Observe before acting

Understanding a system always comes before modifying it.

---

## Individuals remain responsible

Technology assists.

Individuals decide.

Responsibility cannot be delegated.

---

## AI augments people

Artificial intelligence extends human capabilities.

It never replaces human responsibility.

---

## Organizations should trust people

Technology should strengthen collaboration, not surveillance.

---

## Every device deserves a chance

Useful hardware should remain useful.

Obsolescence should not be a business model.

---

## Every technology has value

Modern and legacy technologies can coexist.

Interoperability is more important than age.

---

## One workspace, many ecosystems

Users should experience consistency even when technologies evolve.

---

## Standards build trust

Use standards created for the public interest whenever possible.

---

## Quiet technology

The best technology often goes unnoticed.

It simply works.

---

## Durable software

Software should evolve without forcing unnecessary replacement.

---

## Empower, don't lock in

Technology should increase freedom, not dependence.

---

## Progress through understanding

People cannot improve what they do not understand.

---

## Consistency over fashion

Design trends change.

Principles remain.

---

## Measure value, not consumption

More hardware.

More cloud.

More software.

More AI.

None of these automatically create more value.

---

## Technology serves life

Technology is a means.

Never the purpose.

---

## Build for tomorrow

Every decision should remain meaningful years from now.

Think beyond the next release.

---

## The initiative comes first

Projects evolve.

Repositories change.

Technologies disappear.

The initiative remains.

---

## Leave things better

Every contribution should improve the ecosystem for the next contributor.

---

## Technology should deserve people's trust

Everything begins here.
