# Resources

> *Resources extend the ecosystem through capabilities.*

## What is a resource?

A resource is any entity capable of providing one or more capabilities to the digital ecosystem.

A resource exists independently from openglacier.

It is not created by the ecosystem.

It simply becomes part of it.

Resources may represent physical devices, software, online services or intelligent systems.

Their implementation is irrelevant.

Only their capabilities matter.

---

## Resources already exist

The purpose of openglacier is not to replace existing technologies.

It is to connect them.

Resources already exist around us.

Our phones.

Our computers.

Our NAS.

Our calendars.

Our email providers.

Our databases.

Our AI models.

Our IoT devices.

Our cloud services.

The ecosystem becomes more valuable as these resources learn to communicate.

---

## Resources are first-class citizens

Resources are not plugins.

They are not extensions.

They are not integrations.

Resources are fundamental members of the ecosystem.

Every resource participates through the same object model.

Every resource can communicate.

Every resource can expose capabilities.

Every resource can collaborate.

---

## Resources are represented as objects

Within the workspace, every resource becomes an object.

The object provides a consistent representation regardless of the underlying technology.

Changing a provider should not change how users understand their ecosystem.

The object remains.

The implementation evolves.

---

## Resources expose capabilities

Resources do not expose implementation details.

They expose capabilities.

For example:

An email provider may expose:

- Send
- Receive
- Search

A storage system may expose:

- Store
- Retrieve
- Synchronize

An AI model may expose:

- Analyze
- Summarize
- Translate

Capabilities define what resources contribute to the ecosystem.

---

## Resources communicate through events

Resources continuously inform the ecosystem about meaningful changes.

Examples include:

A device becomes available.

Storage reaches capacity.

A calendar event is created.

An AI model finishes processing.

A backup completes.

Resources communicate facts.

The ecosystem reacts.

---

## Resources remain independent

Resources keep their own lifecycle.

They remain owned by their respective vendors, organizations or individuals.

openglacier never assumes ownership.

It simply provides a common language through which resources collaborate.

---

## Resources can be replaced

No resource is irreplaceable.

Users remain free to replace technologies whenever they choose.

An email provider may change.

A storage system may evolve.

A cloud provider may disappear.

The surrounding ecosystem continues to function.

Resources should be interchangeable whenever possible.

---

## Local and remote are equals

Resources may execute:

- locally
- remotely
- on-premises
- in the cloud
- at the edge

Location does not change their role within the ecosystem.

Capabilities matter.

Not geography.

---

## Resources should be observable

Every resource should answer simple questions.

What are you?

What can you do?

What is your current state?

What happened recently?

Who depends on you?

Can you be trusted?

Resources contribute to understanding.

Not complexity.

---

## Resources collaborate

Resources rarely operate alone.

Storage supports agents.

Agents consume AI.

Missions orchestrate resources.

Organizations manage identities.

Communication transforms isolated technologies into an ecosystem.

---

## Resources evolve

Capabilities may grow.

Implementations may change.

Providers may disappear.

Protocols may evolve.

The ecosystem should continue to function without forcing users to redesign their workspace.

Resources evolve.

The ecosystem adapts.

---

## Resources are connected, not consumed

Modern software often encourages replacing one service with another.

openglacier encourages something different.

Connect.

Understand.

Collaborate.

Improve.

Resources are valuable because they contribute to the ecosystem, not because they belong to it.
