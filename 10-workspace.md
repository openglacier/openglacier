# Workspace

> *A workspace is a living collection of objects, not a collection of pages.*

## What is a workspace?

A workspace is the visible representation of a digital ecosystem.

It is where individuals observe, understand and interact with everything connected to their ecosystem.

Unlike traditional software, a workspace is not organized around pages or menus.

It is organized around objects.

Each object represents a real entity capable of communicating, evolving and collaborating with others.

---

## One workspace, one ecosystem

An individual interacts with a single workspace regardless of the number of connected technologies.

Email providers may change.

Storage systems may evolve.

Artificial intelligence may improve.

Devices may come and go.

The workspace remains familiar.

Technology evolves.

The workspace endures.

---

## Objects, not pages

Traditional applications organize information into pages.

Dashboards.

Settings.

Lists.

Forms.

Navigation.

The openglacier workspace follows a different model.

Everything visible is an object.

An object may represent:

- an individual
- an organization
- an agent
- a resource
- a mission
- a storage system
- a device
- a service

Objects become the primary way people understand their ecosystem.

---

## Living objects

Objects are never static.

They continuously reflect the current state of the ecosystem.

A storage object displays its health.

An agent displays its activity.

A mission displays its progress.

A device displays its availability.

Objects always communicate their current state before users ask for it.

The workspace is alive.

---

## Zoom, don't navigate

Moving through the workspace should feel natural.

Selecting an object does not open another application.

It simply reveals more context.

Every interaction follows the same principle:

Observe.

Select.

Zoom.

Understand.

Each zoom level exposes additional information while preserving context.

Users never feel lost.

---

## Relationships over hierarchy

Digital ecosystems are networks.

Not trees.

Objects are connected through meaningful relationships.

Resources support missions.

Agents collaborate with individuals.

Organizations own identities.

Devices host services.

Storage systems preserve information.

Understanding these relationships is often more valuable than understanding technical details.

---

## Calm by default

The workspace should never compete for attention.

Notifications remain meaningful.

Animations explain changes.

Colors communicate state.

Visual noise is minimized.

The workspace should feel calm, even when the ecosystem behind it is complex.

---

## Honest interfaces

The workspace never hides reality.

Unavailable resources remain visible.

Failed missions remain observable.

Disconnected devices continue to exist.

Failures are explained instead of ignored.

Technology should remain honest.

---

## Built for everyone

The workspace should remain approachable regardless of technical expertise.

Experts should appreciate its transparency.

Beginners should appreciate its simplicity.

People should understand their ecosystem without needing to understand the technologies behind it.

---

## A place for collaboration

A workspace is more than an interface.

It is where individuals, organizations, agents and resources collaborate.

Communication happens through objects.

Actions happen through missions.

Capabilities emerge through resources.

Everything belongs to the same ecosystem.

---

## The workspace belongs to the individual

The workspace is independent from vendors.

Independent from hardware.

Independent from cloud providers.

Independent from technologies.

It evolves alongside its owner.

The ecosystem changes.

The workspace remains.
