# Why

> *Technology should deserve people's trust.*

## Why this initiative exists

Computing has transformed the world.

It has connected people, accelerated innovation and made knowledge accessible on an unprecedented scale.

Yet, somewhere along the way, we started measuring progress by consumption instead of value.

More applications.
More cloud services.
More powerful hardware.
More subscriptions.
More artificial intelligence.

More of everything.

But not necessarily more understanding, more trust or more freedom.

Technology should help people achieve more with less, not require more to achieve the same.

---

## A digital world built to be replaced

Modern computing has become increasingly disposable.

Devices that still work are considered obsolete.
Software is abandoned long before its purpose disappears.
Entire ecosystems depend on services that users neither control nor fully understand.

Innovation is too often associated with replacement instead of improvement.

We believe technology deserves a longer life.

Hardware should remain useful.
Software should remain understandable.
Knowledge should remain transferable.

Progress should build upon what already exists instead of constantly discarding it.

---

## Communication creates intelligence

No individual system can solve every problem.

Specialized tools already exist for communication, collaboration, storage, automation, finance, engineering, healthcare and countless other domains.

Their value does not come from replacing one another.

It comes from their ability to communicate.

Intelligence does not emerge from isolated systems.

It emerges from interactions.

Just as a single neuron is not intelligent on its own, digital systems become more valuable when they exchange information through open, understandable and trusted communication.

---

## Trust cannot be purchased

Trust is not a feature.

It is the result of transparency.

People should understand how their digital ecosystem works.

Organizations should understand where their data flows.

Administrators should understand what systems are doing.

Artificial intelligence should assist without becoming an unquestionable authority.

Trust grows when technology can be observed, inspected and explained.

Not when it asks to be believed.

---

## Digital sovereignty starts with understanding

Digital sovereignty is often reduced to where data is stored.

We believe it starts much earlier.

People cannot control what they do not understand.

Organizations cannot manage risks they cannot observe.

Privacy, security and resilience should emerge from architecture, not from endless configuration.

Understanding creates autonomy.

Autonomy creates sovereignty.

---

## Sustainability is a responsibility

Technology should respect both people and the resources it depends on.

Extending the useful life of existing hardware is often more valuable than replacing it.

Reducing unnecessary complexity is as important as reducing unnecessary energy consumption.

The most sustainable technology is often the one that already exists.

Innovation should not encourage constant replacement.

It should encourage better use.

---

## Open means more than open source

For us, openness is not only about source code.

It is about standards.

It is about interoperability.

It is about transparency.

It is about collaboration.

Open ecosystems allow individuals and organizations to choose their own technologies without sacrificing their independence.

Technology should connect ecosystems, not imprison them.

---

## Why openglacier

openglacier is an initiative built around a simple belief:

Technology should deserve people's trust.

It exists to promote digital ecosystems that are transparent, sustainable and interoperable.

It does not seek to replace existing tools.

It seeks to help them communicate.

It does not seek to make technology invisible.

It seeks to make technology understandable.

If one day initiatives like openglacier are no longer necessary, it will mean the digital world has finally embraced these principles.

Until then, this is our contribution.
